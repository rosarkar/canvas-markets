What we believe

Every group join is a moment of human attention. Right now, all of it goes to waste.

Someone built a Telegram community over years. Wrote the content, answered the questions, kept the conversation going. When a new person shows up and wants in, they solve a CAPTCHA that benefits Google or Cloudflare and nobody else. The group owner gets nothing. The attention spent is gone.

Canvas puts that moment to work. The new member answers a short question matched to the group's topic. The advertiser gets a verified human response with real audience context. The group owner receives USDC. Same ten seconds, different outcome.

The longer-term project is straightforward: group owners should have direct revenue from the communities they built. Not from brand deals they have to chase, not from platform ad splits that require scale they don't have yet. From the membership activity that's already happening.

Crypto is where we're starting because both sides of the market already speak the language. But the goal is bigger. A Taylor Swift fan community, a fitness group, a cooking channel: every topic has group owners doing real work with no way to earn from it. Canvas is building the infrastructure to change that, one community at a time.

Where this started

One of five finalist projects. Selected at The Vault Buenos Aires.

Rohit had been working on the same problem for several years before Canvas had a name: the CAPTCHA is a toll booth that collects human attention and pays it to nobody. The question was how to redirect that value to the people who built the community hosting it.

At DevConnect 2025 in Buenos Aires, EigenCloud and the Ethereum Foundation's Developer Growth team co-organized The Vault, a builder residency for teams working on verifiable applications. Canvas was selected as one of five finalists and presented the protocol architecture to the cohort.

That version ran on EigenLayer. The current version runs on Base mainnet: USDC settlements, a live escrow contract, AI scoring on every response before any payout clears. The mechanism is the same one that got selected in Buenos Aires. The stack is the one that ships.

What we stand for

Four things we won't compromise on.

Group owners get paid directly
The person who spent two years building a 5,000-member DeFi community did real work. Canvas pays them per verified join and per re-verification, straight to their wallet, with no invoice and no waiting on an advertiser's billing cycle.
The task has to be worth doing
A Canvas verification takes ten seconds. That's a real ask. The task has to be matched to the group's topic, relevant to the advertiser's product, and short enough that a genuine human completes it without friction. If the task is bad, the data is bad. We treat task quality as a product constraint.
Context is what makes the data valuable
A DeFi trader ranking protocol responses knows what good looks like. A random Mechanical Turk worker doing the same task doesn't. Canvas ties every response to a verified, topic-matched human. That provenance is the thing AI labs are actually paying for, and it can't be faked after the fact.
The product runs where people already are
No new app. No new wallet. No behavior change for the person joining a group. Canvas runs inside Telegram, through a bot that group owners already know how to add. The verification flow is the only thing that changes, and for the human completing it, the change is ten seconds longer than before.
Where we're going

Telegram first.

Discord, the open web, and every other platform where communities gather have the same join moment. Telegram is where we prove the model before expanding to them.
